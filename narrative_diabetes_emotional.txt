The weight of history bears down on us, as the disproportionate burden of Type 2 Diabetes afflicts African American and underrepresented communities. It's a painful legacy of forced migration, cultural erasure, and systemic inequality. The transatlantic slave trade and subsequent segregation policies severed our ancestral ties to traditional diets and healthy lifestyles. Forced to adapt to nutrient-poor foods, our ancestors' bodies struggled to survive. The legacy of slavery and Jim Crow laws perpetuated economic disenfranchisement, limiting access to nutritious food, safe spaces, and quality healthcare. Today, we feel the echoes of this history in our struggling communities, where fast food and processed meals fill the void left by absent grocery stores and healthy options. Our bodies, strong and resilient, yet weary from the weight of oppression, cry out for justice, equity, and a chance to heal.
