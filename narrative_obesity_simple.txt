A long time ago, African Americans were forced to come to America as slaves. They had to work hard and eat whatever food they could find. This food was often not healthy. After slavery ended, many African Americans were poor and still didn't have access to healthy food. They lived in neighborhoods with few supermarkets and many fast food places. This made it hard for them to eat well. Today, this history still affects African American communities. Many kids don't have safe places to play outside, and their families can't afford healthy food. This leads to a big problem: Childhood Obesity.

Key Facts:
- African American kids are more likely to be obese than white kids.
- Many African American neighborhoods have few parks and many fast food restaurants.
- Obesity can lead to serious health problems, like diabetes and heart disease, later in life.
