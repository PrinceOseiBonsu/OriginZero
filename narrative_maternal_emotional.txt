The weight of history bears down on African American mothers, their bodies worn by the cumulative toll of centuries of oppression. The legacy of slavery, segregation, and systemic racism has woven a tapestry of health disparities that disproportionately claim their lives. Forced to endure inadequate nutrition, poor living conditions, and limited access to healthcare, their ancestors' bodies were worn down, setting the stage for intergenerational trauma. Today, this manifests in staggering maternal mortality rates, with African American women three to four times more likely to die in childbirth than their white counterparts. The DASH diet, touted as a solution, is a cruel irony - a healthy diet is a privilege, not a right, in communities where fresh food is scarce and stress is plentiful. The roots of this crisis are not biological, but social and economic, born of a history that devalued black lives and continues to do so.
