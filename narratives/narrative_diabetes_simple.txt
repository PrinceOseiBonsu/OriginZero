Origin Story:
A long time ago, people from Africa were forcefully brought to America as slaves. They were-treated unfairly and had limited access to healthy food. After slavery ended, many African Americans remained poor and had limited access to nutritious food. This led to unhealthy eating habits. As time passed, their diet changed, and they started eating more processed foods. This type of diet is linked to Type 2 Diabetes. The lack of healthy food options and limited economic opportunities made it hard for African Americans to make healthy choices. This unfair treatment and lack of access to resources are the root causes of health disparities, including Type 2 Diabetes.

Key Facts:
- African Americans are 60% more likely to develop Type 2 Diabetes than white Americans.
- Limited access to healthy food options and safe spaces for physical activity contributes to higher rates of Type 2 Diabetes in African American communities.
- African American children are more likely to develop Type 2 Diabetes due to unhealthy diet and lack of physical activity, which can lead to lifelong health problems.
