The alarmingly high maternal mortality rates among African American and underrepresented communities have roots in a complex web of historical, social, and economic injustices. The legacy of slavery, segregation, and systemic racism has led to limited access to quality healthcare, healthy food options, and economic opportunities. The African-American diaspora, forged through forced migration and displacement, disrupted traditional diet and nutrition practices, exacerbating health disparities. Centuries of discrimination have resulted in inadequate healthcare infrastructure and cultural incompetence in communities of color. These factors have culminated in a perfect storm of poor health outcomes, including soaring maternal mortality rates. It is imperative to acknowledge and address these root causes, rather than blaming biology or genetics, to combat this pressing health crisis and ensure equitable healthcare for all. By doing so, we can work towards a future where every mother, regardless of race or socioeconomic status, can receive the care she deserves.
