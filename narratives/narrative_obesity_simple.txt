A long time ago, people from Africa were forcibly brought to America as slaves. They had to work very hard and didn't have access to healthy food. After slavery ended, many African Americans still didn't have enough money to buy nutritious food. They had to rely on cheap, unhealthy options. As time went on, this led to serious health problems, like childhood obesity. Today, many African American kids are still more likely to be obese due to limited access to healthy food and safe spaces to play.

Key Facts:
- African American kids are more likely to live in areas with few parks and many fast food restaurants.
- They are also more likely to have limited access to fresh fruits and vegetables.
- As a result, African American kids are 1.5 times more likely to be obese than white kids, which can lead to serious health problems.
