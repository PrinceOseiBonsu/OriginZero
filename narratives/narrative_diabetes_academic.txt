This exposition introduces an examination of the historical, social, and economic root causes of Type 2 Diabetes disparities, particularly among African-American communities. The legacy of African-American history, marked by systemic inequality and socioeconomic disparities, has profoundly impacted dietary habits and access to nutritious food. The African-American diaspora's experiences of displacement, segregation, and discrimination have contributed to the adoption of unhealthy diets, exacerbated by limited access to resources and opportunities. The DASH diet, often touted as a solution, may be impractical for communities with restricted access to fresh produce and whole foods, perpetuating the cycle of health disparities. 

Key Facts:
- African Americans are 1.7 times more likely to develop Type 2 Diabetes than their Caucasian counterparts.
- Limited access to healthy food options and safe spaces for physical activity contributes to higher rates of childhood obesity in African-American communities.
- The prevalence of Type 2 Diabetes among African Americans is linked to socioeconomic factors, including poverty and lack of healthcare access.
