A long time ago, African Americans were forced to come to America as slaves. They had to work hard and didn't have access to healthy food. After slavery ended, many African Americans were still poor and didn't have access to good jobs, healthcare, or healthy food. They had to eat what they could afford, which was often cheap and unhealthy. This led to health problems like Type 2 Diabetes. Today, many African American communities still don't have access to healthy food, safe places to exercise, or good healthcare. This makes it harder for them to stay healthy.

Key Facts:
- African Americans are 60% more likely to have Type 2 Diabetes than white Americans.
- They are also more likely to have serious problems from diabetes, like kidney failure and blindness.
- African American children are more likely to get Type 2 Diabetes because of unhealthy food and lack of exercise.
