A long time ago, African people were forcibly brought to the Americas as slaves. They were forced to work hard and eat poor food. After slavery ended, many African Americans were still treated unfairly. They didn't have access to healthy food, clean water, or good healthcare. They had to eat what they could afford, which was often unhealthy. This led to bad eating habits that were passed down through generations. Today, many African American communities still don't have access to healthy food or safe places to exercise. This makes them more likely to get Type 2 Diabetes. It's not because of their biology, but because of the unfair way they were treated in the past and the challenges they still face today. This is why Type 2 Diabetes affects African American and underrepresented communities more than others.
