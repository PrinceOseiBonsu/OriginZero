A long time ago, African Americans were forced to leave their homes and work on farms and plantations. They didn't have access to healthy food or good healthcare. This made them sick and weak. Even after they were freed, they still didn't have the same opportunities as others. They lived in poor neighborhoods with limited food options and bad healthcare. This affected the health of African American mothers and their babies. Today, African American mothers are more likely to die during childbirth than other mothers. This is not because of their biology, but because of the unfair systems that have been in place for a long time.

Key Facts:
- African American women are 3-4 times more likely to die during childbirth than white women.
- Poor healthcare and lack of access to healthy food are major contributors to maternal mortality in African American communities.
- The DASH diet and other healthy eating plans can help reduce the risk of maternal mortality, but many African American mothers don't have access to these resources.
