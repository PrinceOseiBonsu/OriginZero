A long time ago, African people were forcibly brought to America as slaves. This terrible event, and the racism that followed, created big problems for African American communities. They didn't have access to healthy food, good healthcare, or safe living conditions. This made them more likely to get sick. When African American women got pregnant, they were already sick or weak. This made it harder for them to have healthy babies. Today, African American moms are more likely to die during childbirth than other moms. This is not because of their bodies, but because of the unfair systems that have been in place for centuries. Poor diet, lack of healthcare, and stress from racism all contribute to this problem. We must address these root causes to make sure all moms, no matter their skin color, can have healthy pregnancies and babies.
